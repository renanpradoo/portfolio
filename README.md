# Data & Business Operations Portfolio

This repository is a portfolio of projects based on real business problems I worked on across data, operations, billing, asset performance, and renewable energy analytics.

The projects are organized in chronological order, from `00` to `04`, following the way my work evolved over time: from more operational and business-oriented problems to more structured data modeling and technical reconstruction.

They also follow an increasing level of technical complexity. The first projects are intentionally more narrative and focused on business context, decision-making, and operational impact. The later projects include more structured data models, synthetic datasets, SQL scripts, entity relationships, and reproducible examples. Project `04` is closer to the original technical implementation, including SQL queries based on the actual logic used to calculate earnout values from asset performance ratios.

## Why this portfolio is reconstructed

Some of these projects were originally developed in professional environments, using company files, internal spreadsheets, business rules, and operational data that I no longer have access to or cannot share publicly.

Because of that, this portfolio does not try to be a direct copy of the original production materials.

Instead, I used AI assistance to help reconstruct the projects as portfolio case studies: rebuilding documentation, synthetic tables, SQL scripts, diagrams, and visual examples based on the original business problems, decisions, and workflows.

The core ideas, context, problem-solving approach, and business logic reflect my own work and understanding. The reconstructed files are meant to make those projects easier to understand, review, and discuss.

## How to read these projects

This portfolio is best read as a progression.

The early projects show how I dealt with messy operational processes, fragmented data, unclear business rules, billing complexity, and performance monitoring needs.

The later projects show how those experiences evolved into more structured thinking around data modeling, source-of-truth design, SQL, data quality, reporting layers, and performance-based financial calculations.

In short, this portfolio is not just about tools.

It is about how I approach problems:

- understanding the business context;
- finding where the process is breaking;
- structuring messy information;
- creating visibility for decision-making;
- reducing manual work and operational risk;
- translating business rules into data structures;
- using technical tools pragmatically, not just for the sake of complexity.

## Portfolio theme

Most of these projects sit at the intersection of data, operations, and business decision-making.

They are not polished product builds or academic exercises. They come from real operational ambiguity: spreadsheets that became too important, fragmented sources, rules that lived in people’s heads, recurring manual work, and reporting processes that needed more structure.

That is the kind of problem I enjoy solving.

Turning messy business processes into reliable data structures, controls, and decision-support systems.
